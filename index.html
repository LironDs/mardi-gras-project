What is MArdi Gras?

Mardi Gras is a Christian holiday and popular cultural phenomenon that dates back thousands of years to pagan spring and fertility rites. Also known as Carnival or Carnaval, it’s celebrated in many countries around the world—mainly those with large Roman Catholic populations—on the day before the religious season of Lent begins. Brazil, Venice and New Orleans play host to some of the holiday’s most famous public festivities, drawing thousands of tourists and revelers every year.

The Festival

The French Quarter of the city of New Orleans, the largest of the cities of the state of Louisiana in the south of the United States, becomes for two weeks a year a focus of unbridled parties and celebrations. The famous carnival, the Mardi Gras (Fat Tuesday in French), which is held in many places in the world, reaches in New Orleans a height of beauty and joy unmatched anywhere else. The wonderful carnival of New Orleans, which was first started in 1857 by French expatriates, is a global attraction for thousands of locals and tourists who plan their arrival in the city many months in advance.
